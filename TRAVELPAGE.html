Day 1 - Charminar


Constructed in 1591, Charminar is both a monument and mosque. Perched on the east bank of the Musi river, Charminar in Hyderabad represents the city and is officially affiliated as the Emblem of Telangana. Apart from being a famous tourist spot, Charminar holds significant importance with a rich history. Charminar was built by the fifth ruler of the Qutb Shahi dynasty, Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah.

Salar Jung Museum


The Salar Jung Museum, nestled on the banks of the Musi River in Hyderabad, India, stands as a cultural jewel and a testament to historical richness. Named after Salar Jung III, the former Prime Minister of the seventh Nizam of Hyderabad, the museum is a testament to his passion for art and heritage. Boasting an expansive collection, it holds a mesmerizing array of artifacts that spans epochs and civilizations. Among its treasures is the renowned "Veiled Rebecca," a masterpiece sculpted by Giovanni Maria Benzoni, capturing the essence of artistry in the 19th century. The museum extends beyond sculptures, encompassing rare manuscripts, textiles, ceramics, and an intriguing Clock Room featuring antique timepieces from around the world. The Diwan Deodi, part of the museum complex, once the ancestral palace of the Salar Jungs, adds historical charm to the visit. Visitors embark on a journey through time, immersing themselves in the cultural tapestry of India and beyond. With its architectural splendor and diverse exhibits, the Salar Jung Museum offers an enriching experience, inviting patrons to explore the intricate threads of history, art, and heritage woven within its walls.

Day 2 - Ramoji Film City


Home to the world's largest film studio complex according to the Guinness Book of World Records, the magical and magnificent Ramoji Film City has developed into a neighbourhood with its own unique charm that attracts millions of tourists who drive 34 km outskirts of Hyderabad to watch Tollywood movie sets and film actors at work. Spread over 1500 acres, this massive film city is dotted with vast gardens, large kitschy statues, replicas of popular historical monuments, open-air film sets, multi-cuisine restaurants, fast food joints and stylish cafes. You can spend an entire day here and choose to stay back in the 5-star and 7-star hotels in the film city. Head to the hilltop from where you can get spectacular views of the entire film city. On your way back, drive through the nearby Pochampally, a cluster of villages that is famous for its Ikat style of weaving for some traditional Hyderabad handloom shopping.
